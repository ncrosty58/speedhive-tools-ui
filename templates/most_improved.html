How This Is Measured

Each driver's consistency score (CV — lap-time variation as a percentage of their own pace) in their earliest qualifying year (at least {{ min_laps }} laps) is compared to their most recent qualifying year — not a fixed calendar-year pair, so drivers who started at different times are each measured against their own history. Consistency is used rather than raw lap time since it stays meaningful even if a driver's class or track mix changed between the two years. Drivers with only one qualifying year aren't shown — there's nothing to compare them against yet.
